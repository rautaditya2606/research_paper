The results above, specifically for INT8, must be interpreted with the following technical context to ensure academic rigour:

A. INT8 Backend Mismatch (CPU vs. Dedicated Hardware):
   - The INT8 results were generated using ONNX Runtime's Dynamic Quantization on a general-purpose CPU.
   - Observation: INT8 is frequently slower than FP32 in these results (e.g., MobileNetV3 3.4ms -> 12.6ms).
   - Reason: This is a "Backend Mismatch." Unlike dedicated Edge AI hardware (like Jetson Nano's Tensor Cores or DP4A instructions), generic CPU backends often lack optimized INT8 arithmetic pipelines. The overhead of dequantization/repacking during inference exceeds the calculation savings.
   - Conclusion: The test validates model-size reduction (approx. 75% savings) but does not represent true "INT8 Hardware Acceleration."

B. ConvNeXt FP16 Anomaly:
   - ConvNeXt-Tiny shows a significant slowdown in FP16 compared to FP32 on this specific backend. This is likely due to lack of optimized FP16 kernels for the custom LayerNorm/Depthwise-Conv structure on the selected CPU provider.
